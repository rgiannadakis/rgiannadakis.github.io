Rafail Giannadakis (he/him) holds a BA in Philology, specializing in Classical Studies, from the University of Crete. His academic interests center on Greek and Latin poetry, ancient philosophy, and Latin tragedy, with a particular emphasis on the melic poetry of Pindar and Sappho, Platonic philosophy, and Senecan tragedy. His research integrates digital humanities and AI applications in Classics and the humanities more broadly, exploring how these methods can support and advance research, teaching, and humanistic inquiry. Additionally, he is interested in literary representations of women and material culture in antiquity.

Before joining UC Santa Barbara as a PhD student, Rafail worked as a Research Assistant on the TALOS–AI4SSH EU ERA Chair project at the University of Crete, where he contributed to research, publications, and training at the intersection of Classics, digital humanities, and artificial intelligence. At UC Santa Barbara, his work will integrate philological approaches with digital and computational methods to support close reading, textual analysis, and the interpretation of ancient literatures and languages. His studies at UCSB are partially supported by the 2026–2027 Fulbright Foreign Student Program.
